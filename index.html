Key Partners Belangrijke Partners

Example questions: Voorbeeldvragen:

  • Who are our key partners? Wie zijn onze belangrijkste partners?
  • Who are our key suppliers? Wie zijn onze belangrijkste leveranciers?
  • Which key resources are we acquiring from partners? Welke belangrijke middelen verkrijgen we van partners?
  • Which key activities do partners perform? Welke belangrijke activiteiten voeren partners uit?

Key Activities Belangrijke Activiteiten

Example questions: Voorbeeldvragen:

  • What key activities do our value propositions require? Welke kernactiviteiten vereisen onze waardeproposities?
  • What activities are most important for our distribution channels? Welke activiteiten zijn het belangrijkst voor onze distributiekanalen?
  • What activities are required for customer relationships? Welke activiteiten zijn nodig voor klantrelaties?

Value Propositions Waardeproposities

Example questions: Voorbeeldvragen:

  • What value do we deliver to the customer? Welke waarde leveren we aan de klant?
  • Which customer problems are we helping to solve? Welke klantproblemen helpen we op te lossen?
  • What bundles of products and services are we offering? Welke bundels van producten en diensten bieden we aan?
  • Which customer needs are we satisfying? Aan welke klantbehoeften voldoen we?

Business Model Canvas Help

What is a Business Model Canvas?

The Business Model Canvas is a strategic management template for developing new or documenting existing business models. It is a visual chart with elements describing a firm's value proposition, infrastructure, customers, and finances.

How to use this tool:

  • Add notes to each section by clicking the "Add note" button.
  • Drag and drop notes between sections to reorganize your ideas.
  • Click the help icon in each section for guiding questions.
  • Your work is automatically saved in your browser.
  • Create multiple canvases for different business ideas or iterations.

The 9 Building Blocks:

  1. Key Partners: Who are your key partners and suppliers?
  2. Key Activities: What key activities does your value proposition require?
  3. Key Resources: What key resources does your value proposition require?
  4. Value Propositions: What value do you deliver to customers?
  5. Customer Relationships: What type of relationship do you establish with customers?
  6. Channels: How do you reach your customers?
  7. Customer Segments: Who are you creating value for?
  8. Cost Structure: What are the most important costs in your business model?
  9. Revenue Streams: How does your business earn revenue from your value propositions?
